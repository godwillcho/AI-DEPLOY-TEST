DOCUMENT REQUIREMENTS — What to Bring
Stability360 by Trident United Way

When applying for assistance through Stability360 or community partner
programs, you may need to provide certain documents.  Not all documents
are required for every program — the specific requirements depend on
which program you are applying for.

===================================================================
COMMONLY REQUESTED DOCUMENTS
===================================================================

IDENTIFICATION
- Valid photo ID (driver's license, state ID, passport, military ID)
- Social Security cards for all household members being served
- Birth certificates for children (if applying for child-related programs)

PROOF OF INCOME (last 30 days)
- Pay stubs (most recent 2-4 weeks)
- Social Security / SSI / SSDI benefit letter
- Unemployment compensation letter
- Child support documentation
- Self-employment records (profit/loss statement or tax return)
- Pension / retirement income statements
- Zero income affidavit (if you have no income — available at resource centers)

PROOF OF RESIDENCY
- Current lease or rental agreement
- Utility bill in your name (within last 30 days)
- Mortgage statement
- Letter from person you are staying with (if no formal lease)

HOUSING-RELATED
- Eviction notice or court summons (if facing eviction)
- Lease showing monthly rent amount
- Landlord name, phone number, and mailing address
- Move-in cost breakdown (if seeking security deposit help)

UTILITY-RELATED
- Current utility bill showing past-due amount
- Shutoff notice (if applicable)
- Utility account number
- Utility provider name and customer service number

MEDICAL/HEALTH-RELATED
- Health insurance card (if applicable)
- Prescription information (pharmacy name, medication, cost)
- Doctor's note (if unable to work)
- Disability documentation (if applicable)

===================================================================
PROGRAM-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
===================================================================

LIHEAP
- Photo ID
- Social Security numbers for all household members
- Proof of income for all household members (last 30 days)
- Current utility bill
- Proof of residency

SNAP
- Photo ID
- Social Security numbers
- Proof of income
- Proof of residency
- Bank statements (checking and savings)
- Proof of expenses (rent, utilities, child care, medical)

EMERGENCY FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE (Stability360)
- Photo ID
- Proof of income
- Proof of residency
- Documentation of the emergency (shutoff notice, eviction notice, etc.)
- Vendor information (who to pay and how)

===================================================================
DO NOT HAVE DOCUMENTS?
===================================================================
If you do not have some of these documents:
- Tell your case manager — they can often help you obtain them
- Some programs accept alternative forms of verification
- You can still begin the intake process without all documents
- Resource center staff can help you get a state ID if needed
- Zero income affidavits are available at all TUW resource centers

===================================================================
HOW TO PREPARE
===================================================================
1. Gather the documents listed above that apply to your situation
2. Make copies if possible (the resource center can also make copies)
3. Bring documents to your appointment, or have digital copies ready
   to share by email if your intake is remote
4. If you are unsure what to bring, contact your local resource center:
   - Berkeley: BRCC (843) 761-8222
   - Charleston: NCRCC (843) 740-7045 or CRCC (843) 577-3480
   - Dorchester: DRCC (843) 563-2590

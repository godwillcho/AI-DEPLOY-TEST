Financial Wellness Program

The Financial Wellness program is part of the Thrive@Work suite of services provided through Trident United Way's Stability360 platform.

Services Available:
- One-on-one financial coaching sessions with certified financial counselors
- Budgeting and money management workshops (virtual and in-person)
- Debt management planning and credit counseling referrals
- Emergency financial assistance for qualifying employees facing unexpected hardship
- Tax preparation assistance (seasonal, free for qualifying individuals)
- Savings program enrollment and matched savings opportunities

Emergency Financial Assistance:
Employees experiencing a financial crisis (e.g., eviction notice, utility shutoff, medical emergency) may qualify for one-time emergency assistance. Eligibility is based on:
- Active enrollment in Thrive@Work
- Demonstrated financial hardship
- Completion of a brief needs assessment
- Willingness to participate in at least one financial coaching session

Maximum assistance amounts vary by employer partnership level. Typical limits range from $500 to $2,000 per calendar year.

Community Resource Referrals:
The Financial Wellness program also connects employees to community resources including:
- SNAP (food assistance) application support
- LIHEAP (energy assistance) referrals
- Housing assistance programs
- Free or reduced-cost childcare programs
- Transportation assistance

All financial information shared is confidential and is not reported to employers.

Frequently Asked Questions - Thrive@Work

Q: What is Thrive@Work?
A: Thrive@Work is an employer-partnered program operated by Trident United Way that provides employees with confidential access to wellness services, financial coaching, counseling, and community resources. It is available at no cost to enrolled employees.

Q: How do I know if I am enrolled in Thrive@Work?
A: If your employer is a Thrive@Work partner, you should have received an employee ID (format: TW-XXXXX) from your HR department. You can verify your enrollment by providing this ID when you contact us.

Q: Is Thrive@Work confidential?
A: Yes. All interactions with Thrive@Work are strictly confidential. Your employer will not know that you called, what you discussed, or what services you received. The only exception is if there is an immediate safety concern as required by law.

Q: Does it cost anything to use Thrive@Work?
A: No. Thrive@Work services are fully funded by your employer. There is no cost to you for using the helpline, counseling sessions, financial coaching, or community resource referrals.

Q: What if I don't have an employee ID?
A: Contact your employer's Human Resources department to request your Thrive@Work employee ID. If your employer is not currently a Thrive@Work partner, you can still access general community resources through our 211 referral service.

Q: Can my family members use Thrive@Work?
A: Yes, household members of enrolled employees are eligible for select services, including EAP counseling sessions. When you call, let us know you are a household member of an enrolled employee.

Q: How many counseling sessions can I receive?
A: Through the Employee Assistance Program, you may receive up to 6 counseling sessions per issue per calendar year at no cost. If you need additional sessions, your EAP counselor can provide referrals that may be covered by your health insurance.

Q: What if I need help after business hours?
A: Our AI assistant is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. For crisis situations outside of business hours, you will be connected to the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline or local emergency services as appropriate.

Q: Can I request to speak with a person instead of the AI?
A: Absolutely. You can request a human agent at any time during the conversation. Just say "I'd like to speak with a person" and you will be transferred to the next available agent.

Q: What areas does Thrive@Work serve?
A: Thrive@Work primarily serves the Lowcountry region of South Carolina, including Berkeley, Charleston, and Dorchester counties. Some services, such as EAP counseling and financial coaching, may be available virtually regardless of location.

Q: What kinds of help can I get?
A: Thrive@Work can help with a wide range of needs including mental health support, substance use concerns, financial difficulties, housing assistance, food assistance, childcare, transportation, healthcare access, legal consultations, and more. If we cannot help directly, we will connect you with a community organization that can.

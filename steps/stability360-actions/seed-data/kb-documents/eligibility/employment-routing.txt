EMPLOYMENT STATUS ROUTING RULES
Stability360 by Trident United Way

This document defines how the client's employment status affects program
routing and available resources.

===================================================================
FULL-TIME EMPLOYED (above area standard income)
===================================================================
Path: Primarily REFERRAL for community resources
Notes:
  - Client may still qualify for some programs (sliding scale)
  - Focus on budgeting support, financial coaching, community resources
  - If employer is a Thrive@Work partner, employer-based EAP services
    are available through the Thrive@Work agent
Resources:
  - Consumer Credit Counseling
  - Financial coaching (through EAP if employer participates)
  - Community resource connections as needed

===================================================================
FULL-TIME EMPLOYED (at or below area standard income)
===================================================================
Path: MIXED (some direct support, some referral)
Notes:
  - May qualify for LIHEAP, emergency assistance, and some direct support
  - Working poor — income insufficient to cover basic needs
  - Area standard: approximately $4,200/month gross for single adult in
    tri-county SC area
Resources:
  - LIHEAP (if income below 150% FPL)
  - Emergency financial assistance (Stability360 direct)
  - Food assistance (SNAP, food banks)
  - Earned Income Tax Credit awareness

===================================================================
PART-TIME EMPLOYED
===================================================================
Path: MIXED to DIRECT SUPPORT depending on income
Notes:
  - Assess whether part-time is by choice or circumstance
  - If seeking full-time, connect with employment services
  - Likely qualifies for more assistance programs
Resources:
  - SC Works (job search, training, full-time opportunities)
  - SNAP and food assistance
  - Medicaid (income-dependent)
  - Emergency assistance as needed

===================================================================
UNEMPLOYED — ACTIVELY SEEKING
===================================================================
Path: DIRECT SUPPORT + employment referrals
Notes:
  - High priority for financial assistance programs
  - Connect with employment services immediately
  - Assess for unemployment benefits eligibility
Resources:
  - SC Works (mandatory for many assistance programs)
  - SC DEW (unemployment benefits)
  - Emergency financial assistance
  - SNAP and food assistance
  - Medicaid
  - Job training programs (Trident Technical College, Goodwill)

===================================================================
UNEMPLOYED — NOT SEEKING / UNABLE TO WORK
===================================================================
Path: DIRECT SUPPORT
Notes:
  - If unable to work due to disability, route to disability services
  - If not seeking due to caregiving, route to caregiver support
  - Highest need for assistance programs
Resources:
  - Disability: SC Vocational Rehabilitation, SSI/SSDI application help
  - Caregiver: BCDCOG caregiver support, respite care
  - Emergency assistance (all categories)
  - Medicaid
  - SNAP

===================================================================
SELF-EMPLOYED / GIG WORK
===================================================================
Path: MIXED
Notes:
  - Income may be irregular — assess average monthly
  - May need help documenting income for program applications
  - Connect with small business resources if applicable
Resources:
  - SCORE (free business mentoring)
  - SC Small Business Development Center
  - Financial coaching for irregular income management
  - Emergency assistance if income drops

===================================================================
RETIRED
===================================================================
Path: Primarily REFERRAL to senior services
Notes:
  - Assess fixed income adequacy
  - Route to BCDCOG for comprehensive senior support
  - If retirement income is insufficient, may qualify for direct support
Resources:
  - BCDCOG Aging Services
  - Social Security benefits review
  - Medicare/Medicaid counseling (SHIP)
  - Senior food programs
  - Property tax relief for seniors

===================================================================
STUDENT
===================================================================
Path: REFERRAL + limited DIRECT SUPPORT
Notes:
  - Assess if student is dependent or independent
  - Connect with school financial aid
  - Limited emergency assistance may be available
Resources:
  - School financial aid office
  - SNAP (limited eligibility for students)
  - Emergency assistance (case-by-case)
  - Trident Technical College support services

Employee Assistance Program (EAP) Services

What is the EAP?

The Employee Assistance Program is a confidential, employer-funded benefit available to all Thrive@Work enrolled employees and their household members. EAP services are provided at no cost to the employee.

Services Covered

  • Mental Health Counseling: Individual, couples, and family counseling for anxiety, depression, grief, trauma, and other mental health concerns.
  • Substance Use Support: Assessment, counseling, and referrals for alcohol and drug-related concerns.
  • Crisis Intervention: Immediate support for employees experiencing a crisis, including 24/7 access to crisis counselors.
  • Work-Life Balance: Coaching and resources for managing stress, time management, and balancing personal and professional responsibilities.
  • Legal and Financial Consultations: Brief consultations with legal and financial professionals for personal matters.
  • Relationship Support: Counseling for relationship difficulties, parenting challenges, and family conflicts.

Session Limits

Employees may receive up to 6 counseling sessions per issue per calendar year. If additional sessions are needed, the EAP counselor will provide referrals to community providers, which may be covered by the employee's health insurance.

Confidentiality

All EAP services are strictly confidential. Information shared with EAP counselors is not disclosed to employers, supervisors, or any third party without the employee's written consent, except as required by law (e.g., imminent danger to self or others, child or elder abuse).

How to Access

Employees can access EAP services by calling the Thrive@Work helpline, using the online chat portal, or requesting a referral through the Stability360 AI assistant. An employee ID is required for initial verification.

Thrive@Work Eligibility Rules

Who Is Eligible:
- All employees of participating Thrive@Work employer partners
- Employees must have an active employee ID in the Stability360 system
- Part-time and full-time employees are both eligible
- Temporary and seasonal employees may be eligible depending on employer agreement
- Household members of enrolled employees are eligible for select services (EAP counseling)

Verification Process:
1. Employee provides their Thrive@Work employee ID (format: TW-XXXXX)
2. The system verifies the ID against the employer database
3. If matched, the employee's employer, partnership status, and eligible programs are confirmed
4. If not matched, the employee is directed to contact their HR department for enrollment

Employer Partnership Status:
- Active: Full access to all Thrive@Work programs
- Pending: Employer partnership is being set up; limited services may be available
- Inactive: Partnership has ended; employee may still access 211 community resources

Program-Specific Eligibility:
- EAP Counseling: Available to all active employees and household members
- Financial Wellness: Available to all active employees
- Emergency Assistance: Requires active status plus demonstrated financial hardship
- Wellness Programs: Available to all active employees; some programs may have waitlists
- 211 Community Resources: Available to everyone, regardless of enrollment status

Geographic Coverage:
Thrive@Work primarily serves the Lowcountry region of South Carolina, including Berkeley, Charleston, and Dorchester counties. Some services may be available statewide or nationally depending on the provider.

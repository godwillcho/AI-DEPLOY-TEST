24/7 HOTLINES AND CRISIS RESOURCES
Stability360 by Trident United Way

===================================================================
GENERAL HELP
===================================================================

211 (United Way Information & Referral)
Phone: Dial 2-1-1 (or 866-892-2114 from cell phones)
Text: Text your ZIP code to 898-211
Available: 24/7
Services: Connection to local resources for any need — housing, food,
  utilities, health, employment, and more
Website: sc211.org

===================================================================
EMERGENCY
===================================================================

911
Available: 24/7
For: Life-threatening emergencies, crimes in progress, fires, medical emergencies
Note: Always call 911 first for any immediate safety concern

MENTAL HEALTH CRISIS
===================================================================

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
Phone: Dial 988
Text: Text 988
Chat: 988lifeline.org/chat
Available: 24/7
For: Suicidal thoughts, emotional distress, mental health crisis, substance use crisis
Languages: English and Spanish; other languages via interpreter

Charleston/Dorchester Mental Health Center Crisis Line
Phone: (843) 414-2350
Available: 24/7
For: Mental health crisis, psychiatric emergency, substance use crisis
Services: Phone assessment, mobile crisis team, crisis stabilization

National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) Helpline
Phone: 1-800-950-6264
Text: Text "HELPLINE" to 62640
Available: Monday – Friday, 10 AM – 10 PM ET
For: Information, support, referrals for mental health conditions

WHEN TO DIRECT CLIENTS TO THESE RESOURCES
===================================================================
The AI assistant should immediately provide relevant crisis hotline
numbers when a client:
- Expresses thoughts of suicide or self-harm → 988
- Reports domestic violence or fear of a partner → My Sister's House or
  National DV Hotline
- Reports sexual assault → Tri-County SPEAKS or RAINN
- Reports child abuse or neglect → SC DSS Hotline
- Describes an immediate safety threat → 911
- Reports substance use crisis → SAMHSA Helpline or 988
- Is a veteran in crisis → Veterans Crisis Line (988, press 1)

Always provide the resource AND offer to stay on chat or escalate to
a live agent.  Never leave a person in crisis without a clear next step.

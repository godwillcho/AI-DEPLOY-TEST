UTILITY ASSISTANCE PROGRAMS — Stability360 by Trident United Way

OVERVIEW
Stability360 connects eligible individuals and families with utility assistance
programs that help cover past-due balances, prevent shutoffs, and maintain
essential services.  Available programs vary by county, provider, and funding
availability.

PROGRAMS AVAILABLE

1. LIHEAP (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program)
   - Federal program administered through SC Department of Social Services
   - Helps with heating and cooling costs
   - One-time benefit per program year (October – September)
   - Income must be at or below 150% of the Federal Poverty Level
   - Applicants must be responsible for their energy costs
   - Apply through local DSS office or Palmetto Community Action Partnership

2. ShelterNet Utility Assistance
   - Available in Charleston and Dorchester counties
   - Covers electric, gas, and water past-due balances
   - Maximum assistance typically $500 per household per year
   - Must show proof of past-due balance or shutoff notice
   - Contact ShelterNet at (843) 723-4880

3. Santee Cooper Customer Assistance
   - Available to Santee Cooper customers in Berkeley County
   - Operation Round Up — community-funded bill assistance
   - Budget billing to level out seasonal cost swings
   - Flexible payment plans for past-due balances
   - Contact Santee Cooper at 1-888-289-2522

4. BRCC (Berkeley Resource Connection Center) Utility Aid
   - Trident United Way Berkeley County center
   - Emergency utility assistance for Berkeley County residents
   - Typically covers electric past-due up to $300
   - Must bring shutoff notice or past-due bill
   - Walk-in hours: Monday-Friday, 9 AM – 3 PM

EXTENDED INTAKE FIELDS (collected during intake for utility assistance)
   - Utility type: electric, gas, water, sewer, trash
   - Utility provider name
   - Account number (optional)
   - Amount past due
   - Date of shutoff notice (if applicable)
   - Has a shutoff date been issued? (yes/no)
   - Shutoff date (if yes)
   - Has the client contacted the utility provider? (yes/no)
   - Is the client on a payment plan? (yes/no)

PRIORITY ESCALATION RULES
   - Shutoff notice with date within 72 hours → PRIORITY
   - Household includes children under 5 or adults over 65 → PRIORITY
   - Medical equipment requiring electricity → PRIORITY
   - Shutoff has already occurred → PRIORITY

WHAT TO BRING
   - Photo ID
   - Proof of income (last 30 days)
   - Current utility bill showing past-due amount
   - Shutoff notice (if applicable)
   - Proof of address (lease or utility bill)
   - Social Security cards for all household members

CONTACT INFORMATION
   - Berkeley County: BRCC (843) 761-8222
   - Charleston County: ShelterNet (843) 723-4880 or NCRCC (843) 740-7045
   - Dorchester County: ShelterNet (843) 723-4880 or DRCC (843) 563-2590
   - Statewide LIHEAP: SC DSS 1-800-768-5700
   - Dial 211 for general utility assistance referrals

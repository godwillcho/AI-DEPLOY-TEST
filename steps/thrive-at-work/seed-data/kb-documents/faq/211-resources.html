211 Community Resources

Through Trident United Way's 211 network, Thrive@Work connects employees and community members with local service providers across the Lowcountry region. These resources are available to everyone, regardless of Thrive@Work enrollment status.

Housing Assistance

  • Emergency Shelter: One80 Place (Charleston), Windwood Farm Home for Children (Awendaw)
  • Rent Assistance: Charleston County Human Services, Lowcountry Community Action Agency
  • Affordable Housing: Charleston Housing Authority, Humanities Foundation
  • Utility Assistance: LIHEAP (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program), Charleston Water System assistance programs

Food Assistance

  • Food Banks: Lowcountry Food Bank (serves Berkeley, Charleston, Dorchester counties)
  • SNAP Application Help: DSS offices in Berkeley, Charleston, and Dorchester counties
  • Community Meals: Various faith-based and community organizations offer free meals
  • WIC (Women, Infants, Children): DHEC county health departments

Healthcare

  • Free/Sliding Scale Clinics: Fetter Health Care Network, Roper St. Francis Free Clinic
  • Mental Health Services: MUSC Telehealth, Charleston Dorchester Mental Health Center
  • Substance Use Treatment: Charleston Center, Palmetto Lowcountry Behavioral Health
  • Health Insurance Enrollment: Navigators available during open enrollment at local libraries and community centers

Childcare and Family Services

  • Childcare Assistance: SC Voucher Program (DSS), Head Start / Early Head Start
  • After-School Programs: Boys & Girls Clubs of the Lowcountry, YMCA after-school care
  • Parenting Support: Parents as Teachers, Family Resource Centers

Transportation

  • Public Transit: CARTA (Charleston Area Regional Transportation Authority)
  • Medical Transportation: Medicaid transportation services, volunteer driver programs
  • Vehicle Assistance: Working Wheels program (vehicle repairs for qualifying individuals)

How to Access 211 Resources

You can access 211 resources by:

  1. Asking the Stability360 AI assistant about community resources
  2. Dialing 2-1-1 from any phone
  3. Visiting 211.org online
  4. Texting your ZIP code to 898-211
